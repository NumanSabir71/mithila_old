The Mithila Story

2,500 Years of
Sacred Colour

In the Mithila region of Bihar, women have painted the walls of their homes for millennia — at births, weddings, festivals. Each motif carries divine meaning: the lotus for purity, the fish for abundance, the peacock for beauty, the sun for life itself.

Mithila Design was born to carry these sacred brushstrokes beyond the village walls — onto silk, chanderi, and cotton worn by women who carry this art into the world.

Origin of Mithila Art · Bihar, India
Born in Madhubani, Bihar

The Heartland
of Living Art

Madhubani, a small city in Bihar's Mithila region, is where one of the world's oldest unbroken art traditions lives and breathes. For over 2,500 years, the women here have painted every wall, every floor, every sacred surface with the stories of gods, nature, and love.

  • UNESCO-recognized Intangible Cultural Heritage
  • Passed mother to daughter for 2,500+ years
  • Natural pigments from plants, minerals, and earth
  • Sacred motifs each carry mythological meaning
  • 200+ women artisans partnered with Mithila Design

The Language
of Mithila Art

Lotus

Purity, awakening & the divine feminine. Most sacred motif in Mithila.
Fish
मछली
Fertility & abundance. Always painted in pairs to symbolise love.
Peacock

Beauty, royalty & Krishna's divine grace. A timeless Mithila signature.
Sun
