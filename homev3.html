    The Language of Art

    Sacred Mithila Motifs

    Learn More
    The Lotus
    कमल
    Symbol of purity, spiritual awakening, and the divine feminine. The most sacred motif in all Mithila art.
    The Fish
    मछली
    Represents fertility, auspiciousness, and the abundant waters of the Mithila region. Always painted in pairs for love.
    The Peacock
    मोर
    Emblem of beauty, royalty, and Krishna's divine nature. The peacock feather motif is a timeless Mithila signature.
    The Sun
    सूर्य
    Life-giver, divine light, and protector. The sun motif graces every Chhath Puja painting — Bihar's most sacred festival.
    Tree of Life
    वृक्ष
    The cosmic tree connecting earth and heaven, ancestors and descendants. Painted at every Mithila wedding ceremony.

    From Village to Wardrobe

    How Every Piece Comes to Life

    01
    Sourcing the Finest Fabrics
    We begin with the finest handwoven tussar silk, chanderi, and organic cotton — sourced directly from cooperative weavers across Bihar and Bengal. Every thread carries the integrity of ethical craft.
    02
    The Artisan's Sacred Hand
    Each motif — lotus, fish, peacock, sun — is drawn freehand onto the fabric using traditional bamboo pens and natural pigments. No stencils. No templates. Pure expression from centuries of memory.
    03
    Quality & Conscious Delivery
    Every piece is hand-checked, naturally washed, and packaged in recycled cloth bags with a handwritten note from the artisan who made your garment. Art you can trace back to its creator.
